## Findings by severity

### 🟠 R0 — GPS telemetry-logging dropout on the loaner fleet: real walks render with no track / grey steps
Spotted in the control-panel viewer (`www/control/telemetry/`): `z23h`, `bt1t`, `gjtm` show **no GPS track and grey step zones**. **These are real, completed walks** — the `gps` *telemetry* events stopped at the start while positioning kept driving the walk.

Evidence (`z23h`, SM-A515F, 47 min, step 20):
- Only **17 `gps` events, all in the first 1.5 min** (foreground, at the rendez-vous, acc 3 m), then `gps_state: off / service_off (visibility: background)` + `bg_geo_stop`, and **no further `gps` events** — yet *every other* event type (audio, steps) flows for the full 47 min.
- `spot_audio_prepare.distance_to_center` to each **step** marches down the route in real time: step 4 @76 m → 6 @41 → 9 @28 → 11 @24 → 16 @2 → 20 @25, over 45 min. The walker physically arrives at each zone and the step fires by proximity. Audio loads/plays in sequence (`BLOC_04`→`BLOC_05_ICI_Gwen`→…). So positioning is alive; only the `gps` telemetry emission went silent once backgrounded.

So the viewer is correct (`maps.js` draws the track from `gps` fixes that move; there are none after the start) — the **data is incomplete, not the walk.** Same class as the Xiaomi `as7n`/`q7a5` gap (R3) but **worse: logging never resumes**, so the whole track is lost rather than just a mid-gap. Concentrated on the loaner/spare fleet (SM-A515F, SM-A415F, M2101K7AG). `wgpg` is unrelated — a 2.5-min resume-replay blip (0 GPS), not a walk.

**Impact:** real walks are **invisible to the track view and the blackout scan**, and any "did they actually walk?" check based on logged fixes gives false negatives. No walker-facing effect (the walks were fine).
**Action (telemetry):** find why the `gps` telemetry log call goes silent in background on these devices while the step engine still gets positions (likely the `gps` log is gated on a foreground/source path in `geoloc.js` `_callbackPosition`; the background position path that feeds `updatePosition` doesn't emit one). Until fixed, **do not** use logged-fix span as a completion/traversal gate — cross-check `spot_audio_prepare.distance_to_center` / step proximity instead.

### 🟠 R2 — iOS Motion & Fitness **denied** → hard gate dead-end: `fgl3` (iPhone 11 / iPhone12,1, iOS 16.1.2)
`auth_status: 2` (**Denied**) on **all 63** `motion_prompt` attempts over **1h22m**, `activity_available: true` (real hardware), `motion_prompt_early: 0`, no `step_fire` — **the walk never opened.** Recovered operationally by handing the visitor a loaner (one of the SM-A515F step-20 walks).

**This is distinct from the documented bug-2 hang.** Bug-2 (2026-06-01→02 addenda) is auth stuck **NotDetermined** — iOS structurally refusing to *present* the prompt after the Settings round-trip. Here the prompt was already **answered/denied** before this session began (attempt 1 at elapsed 3 ms already `auth_status:2`), and the early-prompt reorder fix did not fire (`motion_prompt_early:0`). Once Motion is Denied, **iOS will not re-present the prompt** — the hard gate then re-prompts 63× to no effect and the walker is fully blocked with no in-app recovery path (only iOS Settings can flip it).
**Action / open question for the team:** for the *Denied* state specifically, the infinite re-prompt loop is a dead-end. Consider (a) detecting `auth_status==2` and routing to explicit "enable Motion & Fitness in Settings" guidance instead of silent re-prompting, and/or (b) confirming whether the early-motion-prompt reorder is even wired on iOS 16 (it didn't fire here). Note another iOS 16 device today (`b0fr`, iPhone14,4 / iOS 16.1.1) **granted** Motion fine — so this is a per-device permission state, not an iOS-16 regression.
